  Akila Maithripala is a PhD student at RMIT University, Melbourne, researching privacy problems and solutions in large language models and distributed AI systems. My work sits at the intersection of network science and information privacy, with a focus on developing topology-aware differential privacy mechanisms for dynamic, decentralised agentic networks. I am also interested in how privacy threats such as membership inference, model inversion, and data extraction attacks manifest in collaborative and federated learning settings. Prior to joining RMIT, I worked as a Research Assistant at the University of Colombo School of Computing, where I also completed my BSc in Information Systems (Hons).
